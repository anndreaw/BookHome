Description

Cambridge IGCSE Chemistry by Bryan Earl and Doug Wilford is an international edition of the commonly-used textbook on Chemistry. The book provides thorough and latest information on the subject. All the core and supplementary issues which have been specified in the IGCSE syllabus have been dealt with in the book in a comprehensive manner.

The book has numerous practical activities in each chapter. Major developments and scientific ideas have been well illustrated. The discovery of the periodic table along with the theories pertaining to acids and bases has been dealt with extensively. There is ample information provided on topics such as instrumental techniques for identifying elements and compounds, chemical tests for identifying metal ions, electrolysis and international environmental concerns.

The book describes the technological applications of chemistry in great detail. The industrial use of enzymes and the use of hydrogen as a fuel is also propounded here. The book includes many research activities so as to encourage the student to think out of the box. Each chapter has numerous practice questions and all the recent IGCSE exam papers are included as well.

The book is recognised by the University of Cambridge International Examinations. Cambridge IGCSE Chemistry is available in paperback and was published in 2002 by Hodder Education.
